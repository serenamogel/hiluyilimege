Levi finished his schooling at the Hebrew Foundation School and pursued higher studies at West Island College from where he graduated as a valedictorian. Devon Levi Career, Style

Devon Levi was introduced to Hockey by his father. Later on, he started playing in clubs and in college and is aspiring to be a successful ice-hockey goaltender. He is mostly known for his speed and technique. Devon measures his players and reads the game so that he can track the puck through the traffic. He also does this thing, where he drops to his knees 15 feet in front of his net during every time-out, bows his head, and uses breathing techniques to reset. Devon plays as a goaltender and mostly takes catches on his left and his NCAA team is Montreal Canadiens.

Devon Levi in Quebec Midget AAA Hockey League

He started his youth career with the St-Louis Lions in the Under-18 Quebec Midget Hockey League which was a part of the Quebec Junior Hockey League during 2016-2019. Devon was the youngest player on the team. His amazing performance won him the Montreal Amateur Athletic Association Governor’s Award for Student-Athlete of the year in 2018, and Daniel Briere for Best Hockey and Academic Achievement. He made it into the MAA First All-Star Team, and he received the Montreal Canadiens’ Foundation Award two times. Levi won a 1500-dollar scholarship for Athletic and Academic performance. His best game might be in 2019 when he set the record by making the most number of saves in 1 hour, 64 saves out of 65 shots. He flourished with the MAAA Ken Dryden Trophy and the Patrick Roy Trophy as the best defensive player.

Devon Levi in Central Canada Hockey League

After his youth career, he began his junior professional career in Ottawa, Ontario, area with the Carleton Place Canadians in the Central Canada Hockey League during the 2019-2020 season. He had a 0.941 % save percentage, 1.47 goals against average with 8 shutouts. He was made the player of the Year, CJHL, MVP, CJHL Top Rookie, and CLJ Top Goalie thanks to his amazing performance. Not just that, but he also got the CCHL MVP, CCHL Top Goaltender, CCHL Rookie of the year, CCHL Top Prospect, and a CCHL First Team All-Star.

Devon Levi Florida Panthers, Buffalo Sabres

During the 2020 NHL draft, Devon was drafted by Florida Panthers in the 7th round. Later on, his NHL rights were traded to Buffalo Sabres by the Florida Panthers. Even though he wanted to play in the NCAA Division I Northeastern University’s Huskies Men’s ice Hockey Team, in the 2020-2021 season, he could not play due to a persisting Rib injury. However, he played in the 2021-2022 season for Northeastern with back-to-back shutouts and made a record for the season. Levi was named Hockey East Pro Ambitions Rookie of the Month in October and November.

Devon Levi International Debut

Levi’s amazing performance at the 2019 World Junior A Challenge an under-20 tournament where he stopped 77 shots out of 80 turned all eyes on him. This led Canada to win a Gold Medal and he was named the tournament MVP. He played for Canada men’s national junior ice hockey team, helping them get a silver medal. Soon after he was named the Best Goaltender of the Tournament by the IIHF Directorate and named to the tournament All-Star Team. He was also nominated to play in Team Canada in the 2022 Olympics but was later not selected.
