Steve Curry Death Cause Unravel

Los Angeles native Steve Curry died due to heatstroke, as per the medical reports.

On July 18, 2023, tragedy struck Death Valley National Park as a 71-year-old man from Los Angeles, Steven Curry, lost his life amid scorching temperatures.

The incident occurred during a hike where Curry collapsed at the Golden Canyon trailhead, succumbing to the extreme 121-degree heat.

Despite the efforts of first responders, he could not be revived. As park rangers investigate the cause of death, it is suspected that the unforgiving heat played a significant role.

What Happened To Steve Curry Before Death?

On that fateful afternoon, Steven Curry embarked on a hike from Golden Canyon to the famous Zabriskie Point, a distance of about two miles.

Earlier in the day, a Los Angeles Times reporter interviewed him at Zabriskie Point, and photographs depicted him wearing sunscreen and a sun hat.

One image showed him seeking shelter beneath a metal interpretive sign, which provided scant relief from the relentless heat.

However, tragedy struck as Curry headed back to the Golden Canyon trailhead, where his vehicle was parked.

Park visitors discovered him lying on the ground at approximately 3:40 p.m. and immediately called 911.

First responders from the Inyo County Sheriff’s Office and the national park arrived swiftly at 3:47 p.m.

They performed CPR and utilized an automated external defibrillator in their desperate attempt to save Curry’s life.

Unfortunately, their efforts were in vain, and Curry passed away on that scorching day.

The oppressive heat played a significant role in Curry’s death. At the time of the incident, the temperature at nearby Furnace Creek stood at a staggering 121 degrees.

However, the actual temperatures within the confines of Golden Canyon were likely even higher due to the canyon walls radiating the sun’s heat.

Curry was survived by his family members, including his wife, children and grandchildren.
