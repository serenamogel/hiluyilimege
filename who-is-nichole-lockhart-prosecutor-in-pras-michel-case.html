Pras Michel (born Prakazrel Samuel Michel) is a Haitian-American rapper, songwriter, producer, and actor. He is best known as one of the founding members of the hip-hop group The Fugees, which also included Wyclef Jean and Lauryn Hill.

The Fugees rose to prominence in the mid-1990s with their second album, “The Score,” which featured hits like “Ready or Not” and “Killing Me Softly.”

Pras Michel contributed to the songwriting, production, and rapping on the album. He also released several solo albums throughout his career, including “Ghetto Supastar” (1998) and “Win Lose or Draw” (2005).

In addition to his music career, Pras Michel has also ventured into acting, appearing in films such as “Mystery Men” (1999) and “High School High” (1996).

He is also involved in philanthropy work, particularly in Haiti, his country of origin, where he founded the Pras Michel Foundation to support education and culture initiatives.
