Early Life

Scott Hall was born on October 20, 1958, in St. Mary’s County, Maryland, USA. He grew up in a military family and spent his early years moving around the country. His interest in wrestling sparked at a young age, and he pursued it passionately.

Professional Wrestling Career

Scott Hall began his wrestling career in 1984 with Championship Wrestling from Florida. He later moved to the American Wrestling Association (AWA), where he gained popularity. However, his career took a significant turn when he joined the WWF in 1992.

World Wrestling Federation (WWF)

Scott Hall, under the ring name Razor Ramon, became a prominent figure in WWF. His “bad guy” persona and unique wrestling style made him a fan favorite. He won the Intercontinental Championship four times during his tenure with WWF.

World Championship Wrestling (WCW)

In 1996, Hall moved to WCW, where he co-founded the New World Order (nWo), one of the most influential factions in wrestling history. His time in WCW was marked by multiple tag team and United States Championship wins.

Return to WWE and Retirement

Scott Hall returned to WWE in 2002 but his tenure was short-lived due to personal issues. He officially retired from professional wrestling in 2010.
