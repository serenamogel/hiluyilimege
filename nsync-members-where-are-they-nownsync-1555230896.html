NSYNC Members Where Are They Now?

Posted by Martina Birk on Monday, January 1, 0001
NSYNC members are currently engaged in various pursuits: Justin Timberlake in acting and music, JC Chasez in acting and judging, Lance Bass in podcasting, Joey Fatone in acting and stage, and Chris Kirkpatrick in music, acting, and reality TV.

NSYNC

NSYNC, an American boy band, was formed in 1995 and became a pop sensation with members Justin Timberlake, JC Chasez, Chris Kirkpatrick, Joey Fatone, and Lance Bass. They gained international fame with their self-titled debut album in 1997 and achieved record-breaking success with "No Strings Attached" in 2000. Despite legal battles, they sold over 70 million records, earning Diamond certifications for their albums.

Hits like "It's Gonna Be Me" and "Pop" topped charts. After a hiatus in 2002, they sporadically reunited, notably at the 2013 MTV VMAs and receiving a Hollywood Walk of Fame star in 2018. In 2023, the group officially reunited to release the single "Better Place" for the animated film "Trolls Band Together," marking their return to the music scene.

What Does NSYNC Stand For?

NSYNC doesn't actually stand for a specific phrase or set of words. The name is a playful combination of the last letters of the initial members' names: JustiN, ChriS, JoeY, JasoN, and JC. Justin Timberlake's mother is credited with suggesting the name after commenting on how "in sync" the group's singing voices were.

So, NSYNC is more like a creative and catchy representation of the individual members' names coming together harmoniously. The name reflects the unity and synchronization of their voices, contributing to the overall success and popularity of the boy band.

When Did NSYNC Split Up?

NSYNC officially split up in 2004. After releasing their fourth studio album, "Celebrity," in 2001 and completing the corresponding "PopOdyssey" tour, the members decided to take a temporary break in 2002. Despite occasional reunions and appearances over the years, the band did not release new music until September 2023 when they dropped the single "Better Place" for the animated film Trolls Band Together.

Lance Bass, in his 2007 memoir, confirmed that the official breakup of NSYNC occurred in 2004, marking the end of their active music career as a group.

NSYNC Reunion

NSYNC, the famous boyband with members Justin Timberlake, Lance Bass, Joey Fatone, Chris Kirkpatrick, and JC Chasez, has come back together after more than 20 years. They reunited to release a new song titled "Better Place," marking their first single since 2002. This joyful comeback is part of the soundtrack for the animated movie Trolls Band Together.

The song is a catchy pop track where NSYNC showcases their harmonious vocals, and it talks about the excitement of falling in love. The song, along with their reunion, has brought back fond memories for fans, making the world feel like a "better place" with NSYNC's music once again
