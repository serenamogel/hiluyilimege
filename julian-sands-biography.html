Julian Sands Bio (Wiki)

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Saturday, February 17, 2024

Who is Julian Sands?

Julian Sands is an actor from England who earned mainstream fame for his role in the miniseries ‘The Sun Also Rises.

Over the course of his three-decade-long career, he has accumulated about 140 film and TV credits.

A native of the market town of Otley, Sands began his acting career on stage and was associated with the Forum Theatre Company.

How many siblings does Julian Sands have?

Julian Sands was born in Otley, West Riding of Yorkshire, England on January 4, 1958.

His birth name is Julian Richard Morley Sands and he is currently 65 years old.

His father’s name is William Sands and his mother’s name is Brenda Sands.

He has a sibling namely, Quentin Sands. Julian holds British citizenship and English ethnicity. His birth sign is Capricorn.

Education, School/ College University

Talking about Julin’s education history, he attended The Royal Central School of Speech and Drama.

Then, he attended Lord Wandsworth College.

Julian Sands: Professional Life, Career

Talking about his profession, Julian Sands began his film career appearing in supporting roles in films, including Oxford Blues (1984) and The Killing Fields (1984).

Likely, he also had a cameo as a Greek soldier in the BBC adaptation of The Box of Delights in 1984.

He was also cast as the romantic lead in the 1985 film A Room with a View.

In the year 1985, following the success of A Room with a View and Ken Russell’s Gothic (1986) Sands decided to move to Hollywood and pursue a career in American films.

Also, he appeared in several of them—both lower-tier and higher-budget.

Additionally, he played the title role in the horror film Warlock (1989) and its sequel, Warlock: The Armageddon (1993), the role of Franz Liszt in Impromptu (1991), as well as in Boxing Helena(1993) and Leaving Las Vegas (1995).

Likewise, he played “Erik, aka the Phantom” in the 1998 horror-film version of The Phantom of the Opera.

He also starred opposite Jackie Chan in the action-comedy film The Medallion (2003).

And he also played Lord Olivier in BBC Fours In Praise of Hardcore (2005), a drama about the critic and impresario Kenneth Tynan.

In the year 2001, he starred in Stephen King‘s Rose Red. Sands portrayed Austrian ambassador Klemens von Metternich in the 2002 miniseries Napoléon.

During the 2006 season of 24, he played terrorist Vladimir Bierko.

Sands played Jor-El, Superman’s biological father on Smallville, and reprised the role in the series’ final (tenth) season.

In the year 2009, he played Reg Hunt in Bollywood Hero.

In 2012, he played Alistair Wesley in the seventh episode of the second season of Person of Interest.

In 2011, he appeared onstage at the Edinburgh Fringe Festival in A Celebration of Harold Pinter, directed by John Malkovich at the Pleasance Courtyard.

Additionally, his performance in A Celebration of Harold Pinter was well-received; one theatre, the Irish Repertory Theatre, added an extra three weeks to the show’s run.

In 2012, Sands voiced the character of DeFalco in Call of Duty: Black Ops 2.

Julian Sands: Awards, Nomination

He was nominated for Best Actor for Warlock (1989) at Chainsaw Award.

He won The Maiden and the Princess (2011) at Special Jury Award.
